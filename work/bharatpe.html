Overview

Designing financial tools for India's underserved merchant ecosystem

BharatPe serves over 100 million merchants across India — shopkeepers, vendors, small business owners who are deeply familiar with cash but new to digital financial products. The design challenge isn't just usability. It's trust, language, literacy and context.

Across my tenure I led design on four major launches: a 0-to-1 Mutual Funds platform, Credit Line on UPI, Auto EMI Credit Card experience, and a complete redesign of the TPAP app onboarding journey.

Project 01

0-to-1 Mutual Funds Platform

The Problem

BharatPe merchants had savings but no accessible investment vehicle. Mutual funds existed in India but felt inaccessible — complex language, intimidating interfaces, no trust signals for first-time investors.

The Outcome

Launched 0-to-1, acquiring 15K investors within the first 2 weeks — surpassing the estimated 10K target by 50%.

Project 02

Credit Line on UPI — Featured at Global Fintech Fest 2025

The Problem

Credit access for small merchants in India is fundamentally broken — slow, collateral-heavy, and exclusionary. UPI had the reach but no credit layer on top of it.

The Outcome

Designed and launched Credit Line on UPI, featured at Global Fintech Fest 2025, unlocking a $1 Trillion addressable market.

Project 03

Auto EMI Credit Card Experience

The Problem

Daily card subscriptions were stuck at 10 — merchants weren't seeing enough value to commit to a recurring payment product.

The Outcome

Redesigned the Auto EMI Credit Card experience, increasing daily card subscriptions from 10 to 100 — a 10x growth.

Project 04

TPAP App Onboarding Redesign

The Problem

The onboarding journey was taking 12 minutes on average and converting at only 73.31%. Merchants were dropping off before they even started.

The Outcome

Redesigned end-to-end — funnel conversion improved to 85% and average onboarding time dropped from 12 minutes to 5 minutes.
